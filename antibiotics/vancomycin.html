Vancomycin Neonatal Dosage Guidance

Dosing: Dosing presented in mg/kg/dose and mg/kg/day; routes of administration may vary (IV, oral, intrathecal); use caution.

General Dosing

Note: Determination of dosing interval requires consideration of multiple factors including concomitant medications (e.g., ibuprofen, indomethacin), birth depression, hypoxia/asphyxia, cyanotic congenital heart disease, and ECMO status. Monitor serum concentrations closely.

Indication-Specific Dosing

  • Meningitis
    • PNA ≤7 days and ≥2 kg: 20–30 mg/kg/day divided every 8–12 hours
    • PNA >7 days and ≥2 kg: 30–45 mg/kg/day divided every 6–8 hours
  • Ventriculitis / CSF Shunt Infections
    Intraventricular/intrathecal: 3–20 mg/day (typically 5 mg sufficient for neonates)

Dosing Consideration in Altered Kidney Function

In neonates with urine output <1 mL/kg/hour or significantly elevated serum creatinine, consider single-dose administration with serum monitoring rather than scheduled dosing.
