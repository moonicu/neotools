Cefepime Neonatal Dosing Guidelines

General dosing, susceptible infection: Limited data available:

Age-directed dosing

Note: Dosing based on pharmacokinetic evaluations and modeling from neonates and young infants, including patients with GA ranging from 22 to 42 weeks.

Postnatal Age Dose
<30 days 30 to 50 mg/kg/dose every 12 hoursa
≥30 days 50 mg/kg/dose every 8 hoursb

Weight-directed dosing

Body Weight Postnatal Age Dose
≤2 kg ≤28 days 30 mg/kg/dose every 12 hours
≤2 kg 29 to 60 days 50 mg/kg/dose every 8 hoursa
>2 kg ≤28 days 50 mg/kg/dose every 12 hours
>2 kg 29 to 60 days 50 mg/kg/dose every 8 hoursa

aIf pathogen MIC is ≥8 mg/L, doses may need to be infused over 3 hours or frequency increased to every 6 hours.

bIf pathogen MIC is ≥8 mg/L, doses may need to be infused over 3 hours or frequency increased to every 6 hours to achieve desired time > MIC.

ECLS/ECMO Considerations

Components of ECLS/ECMO circuit and pathophysiological changes during critical illness influence the pharmacokinetics of a drug. Monitor patient response closely and refer to institutional protocols.

Administer medication via the patient's vascular access when possible. If unavailable, ECLS/ECMO circuit administration is acceptable.

Infection Treatment

No general dosage adjustment is typically recommended initially. Adjust based on patient-specific factors (e.g., birthweight, kidney function, site of infection, pathogen MIC), clinical response, and therapeutic drug monitoring.

Concomitant Therapy

Significant drug interactions exist. Consult drug interaction databases for appropriate adjustment or avoidance.
