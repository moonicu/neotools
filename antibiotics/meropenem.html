Dosage Adjustment for ECMO (Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation)

Components of ECMO circuit and pathophysiological changes during critical illness influence drug pharmacokinetics. Factors include:

  • Type and age of oxygenator and tubing
  • Blood flow rate through the circuit
  • Organ function (kidney/liver), systemic inflammatory response
  • Significant interpatient variability has been described

Administration via the patient’s vascular access is preferred. If unavailable, direct administration into the ECMO circuit is an acceptable alternative. Monitoring is essential.

General Recommendations

  • Consult institutional protocols for specific adjustments.
  • Adjust dose based on renal function, MIC, and serum concentration monitoring if available.
  • Use therapeutic drug monitoring (TDM) where applicable.
  • Watch for significant drug interactions; consult interactions database.
